Fully Automated Segmentation of Head CT Neuroanatomy Using Deep Learning

Jason Cai, Kenneth Philbrick, Zeynettin Akkus, Bradley Erickson

Radiology Informatics Lab, Mayo Clinic, Rochester MN

 

[GitHub Download] [Paper]

 

 

                                          

 

Abstract

 

Semantic segmentation of the brain is an important prerequisite for diagnosis and treatment planning. We present a U-Net that segments 11 intracranial structures on head CT. Our model generalized to external scans from the RSNA Hemorrhage Detection Challenge, as well as scans demonstrating idiopathic normal pressure hydrocephalus. Overall Dice coefficients were comparable to expert annotations and higher that of existing segmentation methods. In the future, we will include additional intracranial structures and disease states, and we will revalidate our model using the same framework.
 
 
                                        
 
 

Dataset

 

Primary Dataset (Training, Validation and Testing)

  · 62 normal non-contrast head CTs. Mean patient age: 73.4 years old (range: 27-95).

 

Secondary Datasets (Testing)

   · 12 non-contrast head CTs demonstrating idiopathic normal pressure hydrocephalus (iNPH). Mean patient age: 74.3 years old  (range:  0-84).

   ·  30 normal non-contrast head CTs from the RSNA Hemorrhage Detection Challenge (1).

 

Dataset annotation

All slices were annotated using RIL-Contour (2) by a team of trained analysts and supervised by a neuroradiologist.

To protect patient privacy, the datasets are not available for download.
